Global Petrol Prices & GDP in 2022

Data Visualisation 2 - Jordan Trubiano

Many factors have impacted the rising costs on the sourcing and buying of petrol over the years.
This research is based on a global dataset that reviews the costs and factors surrounding petrol in 2022...


Global Price Per Liter of Petrol ($US) 2022

Petrol Vs GDP

The comparison between these top ten countries in categories of Price Per Litre ($US) and Highest GDP show that having higher GDP does not link to countries with expensive fuel. In fact from closer analysis, only Norway appears in both graphs and Iceland, possibly because it is harder to source fuel in those countries, similar to Tonga and Niue which are islands that can make it harder to source.

World Share

World Share represents the amount of petrol shared among each country represented as a percentage to show how much compared to other countries they share of petrol as a resource.

Data shows that United States shares the greatest share of petrol with 20% followed by China and then countries like Russia and Saudi Arabia who are large petroleum producers due to their land containing large oil deposits.


Fuel Consumption

Fuel Consumption is represented by the yearly gallons used per capita in each country every year. Linking that to GDP Per Capita, it shows that GDP does not really have an affect on the amount of gallons used yearly with many countries sitting closer together and only some outliers like Singapore, having a high GDP of 59,798 and highest yearly gallon usage of 3679.5. However, there is no real correlation between having a high or low GDP linking to its effect on yearly gallon usage. In relation to Australia, it sits in the higher section with 704.3 gallons used and a GDP of 51,693.



Average Petrol Price

The Average Petrol Price is measured to be $1.40 across the globe with North Korea standing out with a high of $14.50 and Libya having only 3 cents. In terms of Australia, it sits very close to the average with $1.38 making it very reasonable and quite affordable compared to other countries who may have it harder.
